MATH 3316: Final Exam Review

Part I: Definitions (Memorize Verbatim)

Be able to state these precisely and all related versions. negations are also fair game.

The Real Number System

  • Completeness Axiom: Every nonempty set of real numbers that is bounded above has a least upper bound (supremum).
  • Supremum/Infimum: The precise definitions of least upper bound and greatest lower bound.
  • Convergence (Sequence): A sequence \((x_n)\) converges to \(L\) if for every \(\epsilon > 0\), there exists an \(N \in \mathbb{N}\) such that for all \(n \ge N\), \(|x_n - L| < \epsilon\).
    Negation: Be able to state what it means for a sequence not to converge to \(M\).
  • Divergence to \(\pm \infty\): Definition of a sequence diverging to \(-\infty\) (using \(M < 0\)) or \(+\infty\).

Continuity & Differentiation

  • Continuity (Sequential): A function \(f\) is continuous at \(a\) if for every sequence \((x_n)\) converging to \(a\), the sequence \((f(x_n))\) converges to \(f(a)\).
  • Continuity (\(\epsilon-\delta\)): \(f\) is continuous at \(a\) if for every \(\epsilon > 0\), there exists \(\delta > 0\) such that if \(|x - a| < \delta\), then \(|f(x) - f(a)| < \epsilon\).
  • Uniform Continuity: \(f\) is uniformly continuous on \(S\) if for every \(\epsilon > 0\), there exists \(\delta > 0\) such that for all \(x, y \in S\), \(|x - y| < \delta \implies |f(x) - f(y)| < \epsilon\).
  • Differentiability: \(f\) is differentiable at \(a\) if the limit \(f'(a) = \lim_{x \to a} \frac{f(x)-f(a)}{x-a}\) exists and is finite.
  • Subsequential Limit: A number \(J\) is a subsequential limit of \((s_n)\) if there exists a subsequence \((s_{n_k})\) that converges to \(J\).

Part II: Statements to Know & Apply

Be able to state these theorems and apply them. Proofs of the theorems themselves are secondary here.

Key Theorems

  • Bolzano-Weierstrass Theorem: Every bounded sequence has a convergent subsequence.
  • Monotone Convergence Theorem: Every bounded monotone sequence converges.
  • Intermediate Value Theorem (IVT): If \(f\) is continuous on \([a,b]\) and \(y\) is between \(f(a)\) and \(f(b)\), there exists \(c \in (a,b)\) such that \(f(c)=y\).
  • Extreme Value Theorem: If \(f\) is continuous on a closed interval \([a, b]\), \(f\) attains a maximum and minimum.
  • Rolleâ€™s Theorem / Mean Value Theorem (MVT): Be able to state these accurately regarding differentiable functions on \((a,b)\).

Conceptual Distinctions

  • Continuity vs. Uniform Continuity: Explain the difference in the dependency of \(\delta\). (In uniform continuity, \(\delta\) depends only on \(\epsilon\), not on the point \(a\)).
  • Limit of Function vs. Continuity: Discuss similarities and differences between the definition of \(\lim_{x \to a} f(x)\) and continuity at \(a\).
  • Completeness in \(\mathbb{Q}\): Explain why the Completeness Axiom does not hold in the rationals (e.g., sets bounded above by \(\sqrt{2}\)).

Part III: Examples & Counterexamples

Be able to construct these or explain why they cannot exist.

Sequences

  • A sequence that diverges, but does not diverge to \(\pm \infty\) (e.g., \((-1)^n\)).
  • A bounded divergent sequence.
  • A sequence of positive numbers converging to a negative limit (Impossible).
  • Two sequences where \(s_n \to 0\) and \(t_n\) diverges, but \(s_n t_n \to 0\).
  • A sequence that is neither strictly increasing nor strictly decreasing.
  • A sequence with at least 3 subsequential limits.

Sets and Functions

  • A set with no supremum (e.g., \(\mathbb{R}\)) or explain why one can't exist.
  • A set with a finite infimum but no finite supremum.
  • A set with a supremum but no maximum (e.g., \((0,1)\)).
  • A function that is not continuous at a specific point (e.g., piecewise definition).

Part IV: Proofs (The "Core" Skills)

Group A: \(\epsilon-N\) and \(\epsilon-\delta\) Constructions

  1. Sequence Convergence: Prove convergence for rational functions.
    Example: Prove \((\frac{3n+1}{n+2}) \to 3\).
    Example: Prove \((\frac{2n}{5n+4}) \to 2/5\).
  2. Pointwise Continuity: Use \(\epsilon-\delta\) to prove continuity for polynomials.
    Example: Prove \(f(x)=x^2+4\) is continuous at \(x=3\).
  3. Uniform Continuity: Prove a function is uniformly continuous on an interval.
    Example: \(f(x)=\frac{1}{x^2}\) on \([1, \infty)\).
    Example: \(f(x)=\sqrt{x}\) on \([1, \infty)\).
  4. Discontinuity: Prove a function is discontinuous at a point.
    Example: Dirichlet-style function \(h(x) = x^2\) for \(x \in \mathbb{Q}\), \(0\) for irrational; prove discontinuous at \(x \neq 0\).
  5. Discontinuity: Prove a function is discontinuous at a point.
    Example: \(f(x)=x-3\) for \(x < 2\) and \(f(x)=4-2x\) for \(x \geq 2\).

Group B: Theoretical & Recursive Proofs

  1. Recursive Sequences: Given a recurrence relation (e.g., \(s_{n+1} = \frac{n}{n+1}s_n^2\)):
    List first few terms. Prove convergence (Monotone Convergence Theorem). Compute the limit.
  2. Limit Laws: Prove that if \(s_n \to s\) and \(t_n \to t\), then \(s_n - t_n \to s-t\) (or sum/product).
  3. Supremum Properties:
    If \(S\) is bounded below but has no minimum, prove there exists a sequence in \(S\) converging to \(\inf(S)\).
    If \(A \subset B\), prove \(\sup(A) \le \sup(B)\).
  4. Intermediate Value Theorem:
    Apply IVT to show a root exists (e.g., \(\frac{x}{2} = \sin(x)\)).
    Given \(f(a) \ge g(a)\) and \(f(b) \le g(b)\), prove \(f(c) = g(c)\).
  5. Differentiation: Prove the Product Rule or differentiability of a piecewise function at 0.
  6. Subsequences: Prove every sequence has a monotonic subsequence.
